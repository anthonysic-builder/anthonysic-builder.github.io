Historical Overview

The World War II service of Pvt. Paul Costa reflects the massive logistical effort required to sustain the Allied victory. His journey began as a heavy truck driver with the 8th Air Force in England, hauling munitions and supplies for the strategic bombing campaign.

As the war in Europe culminated, he transferred to the 257th Engineer Combat Battalion (7th Army), serving in Germany during the final days of the war and the subsequent Occupation, helping to rebuild a shattered continent.

Service Timeline

March 22, 1943

Induction

Camp Lee, Virginia. Entered Active Service. Completed Quartermaster School, qualifying as a Heavy Truck Driver (MOS 031) and Mechanic.

1944 – Early 1945

Overseas: Phase I

Watton, England. Attached to the 52nd Station Complement Sq. Responsible for heavy transport convoys supporting 8th Air Force operations.

May 2, 1945

Combat Unit Transfer

Germany. Transferred to the Army Ground Forces. Joined the 257th Engineer Combat Battalion just 6 days before V-E Day.

May – Nov 1945

Occupation Duty

Sulzbach & Marburg, Germany. Served with the 257th ECB assisting in infrastructure repair, bridge building, and logistics in the American Occupation Zone.

November 16, 1945

Separation

Fort Belvoir, Virginia. Honorably Discharged from the Army of the United States.
