Historical Overview

The World War II service of Paul Costa spans two distinct phases of the European conflict. Entering active service in March 1943, he first served as a heavy vehicle operator with the 3rd Strategic Air Depot (8th Air Force), based at Watton, England. For over a year, he drove supply convoys essential to the strategic bombing campaign.

In October 1944, he deployed to France with the 19th Mobile Repair & Reclamation Squadron, supporting forward airfields at Coulommiers and Valenciennes before returning to England to continue logistical support duties.

In early 1945, responding to critical manpower needs, he transferred to the Ground Forces. After infantry retraining, he joined the 257th Engineer Combat Battalion (7th Army) in Wassertrüdingen, Germany. He served through V-E Day and the subsequent occupation, utilizing his logistics skills to assist in the reconstruction of the American Occupation Zone.

Service Chronology

Source: Morning Reports & WD AGO Form 100

22 Mar 1943

Induction

Camp Lee, Virginia

Entered Active Service. Completed Quartermaster School. Qualified as Truck Driver, Heavy (MOS 931). Subsequently sent to Greenville Army Air Base, SC, for specialized Fire Fighter training, indicating initial assignment to crash-rescue operations.

08 Jan 1944

Strategic Air Depot

Watton, England

Arrived in European Theater. Assigned to 52nd Station Comp. Sq (3rd Strategic Air Depot). As a heavy truck driver, executed long-haul convoy missions from ports in Scotland to airfields in East Anglia, transporting aircraft engines, munitions, and rations to sustain the bombing campaign.

19 Oct 1944

Forward Deployment

Coulommiers & Valenciennes, France

Deployed to the continent with the 19th Mobile Repair & Reclamation Squadron. Operated from forward tactical airfields (Stations B-58 & A-83) near the Belgian border, supporting the salvage and repair of aircraft closer to the front lines. Returned to England Nov 18, 1944.

28 Jan 1945

Reclassification

12th Reinforcement Depot

Officially relieved from the Air Corps and transferred to the "Repl Depple" pipeline. This marked a critical transition during the manpower crisis following the Battle of the Bulge, where support personnel were retrained as infantry/engineers for the final assault on Germany.

03 May 1945

Combat Engineers

Wassertrüdingen, Germany

Assigned to H&S Company, 257th Engineer Combat Battalion just five days before the end of the war. On V-E Day (May 8, 1945), he was stationed in Aichach, Germany, celebrating the victory he had helped support logistically for two years.

Summer 1945

Germany

Occupation Duty

Served in the American Occupation Zone at Nördlingen and Sulzbach. Promoted to PFC on May 31, 1945. The unit was engaged in rebuilding civilian infrastructure and maintaining order. In August 1945, he transferred to the Marburg depot for medical treatment before shipping home.

16 Nov 1945

Separation

Fort Belvoir, Virginia

Honorably Discharged.
